Volkswagen

History: Volkswagen, founded in 1937, literally means “People’s Car.” From the Beetle to the Golf GTI, VW has combined practicality, quality, and Euro vibes for decades. It survived scandal and kept evolving.

Relations: VW Group owns Audi, Porsche, Lamborghini, Bentley, Bugatti, and more. It’s one of the largest auto groups in the world, sharing platforms and tech across brands like a boss.

Most Famous Car Models of Volkswagen:

The Beetle is a true icon. Originally a Nazi-era project, it became a peace-loving, hippie symbol. Cute, round, and full of cultural clout.

The GTI is the hot hatch blueprint. Punchy turbo, clean design, and practical size made it the go-to for spirited driving with everyday usability.

The Passat is VW’s mature side—executive vibes with German build quality. It’s the family sedan that doesn’t scream but still flexes subtly.
