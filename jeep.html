Jeep

History: Born during WWII, Jeep became a symbol of freedom and durability. It pioneered the SUV segment and remains one of the most iconic off-road brands in the world.

Relations: Jeep is part of Stellantis and shares components with Dodge, Chrysler, and Fiat. Its rugged DNA still stands out across shared platforms.

Most Famous Car Models of Jeep:

The Wrangler defines off-road freedom. With its removable doors and top, 4x4 capability, and military roots, it’s the go-to for adventure lovers who don’t care for smooth roads.

Luxury meets muscle. The Grand Cherokee blends upscale interiors with serious trail-rated toughness, making it perfect for both urban swagger and rugged escapes.

The Gladiator is Jeep’s truck—literally. It mixes Wrangler's style with a pickup bed, making it a beast for hauling, towing, and looking wild in the wilderness.
