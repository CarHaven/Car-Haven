Bugatti

History: Bugatti was founded in 1909 by Ettore Bugatti in France. It gained fame for its engineering precision and race car dominance. In the modern era, it's known for hypercars like the Veyron and Chiron—blending extreme speed with elegance.

Relations: Bugatti was acquired by the Volkswagen Group in 1998. In 2021, it entered a joint venture with Rimac, forming Bugatti Rimac, with tech influences from Porsche as well.

Most Famous Car Models of Bugatti:

The Veyron shattered speed records and redefined what a road car could be. With a W16 quad-turbo engine and a top speed over 400 km/h, it became the king of hypercars.

Chiron pushed the Veyron’s legacy even further—more power, more luxury, and even more exclusivity. It’s the ultimate blend of craftsmanship and sheer engineering might.

The Divo is Bugatti’s track-focused beast. Sharper aerodynamics, less weight, and breathtaking design make it a collector’s dream and a performance marvel.
