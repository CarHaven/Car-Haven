Dodge

History: Dodge was founded in 1900 and became known for its bold American muscle. From the Charger to the Challenger, Dodge represents raw power and aggressive styling with roots deep in Detroit’s legacy.

Relations: Dodge is part of Stellantis, alongside Chrysler, Jeep, and Ram. It shares engines and platforms with these brands but focuses heavily on performance-driven vehicles.

Most Famous Car Models of Dodge:

The Charger blends classic muscle with modern tech. Four doors, aggressive stance, and engines that snarl—especially the SRT Hellcat trim.

A two-door icon. The Challenger roars old-school muscle vibes but hides insane modern horsepower under the hood—especially in its Demon form.

A performance SUV that says “I’ve got kids... and I can still smoke you at a red light.” The Durango SRT is all bark and bite.
