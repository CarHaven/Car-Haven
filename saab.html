Saab

History: Saab originated from Swedish aerospace engineering in 1945, turning jet-like thinking into road cars. Known for innovation, turbocharging, and quirkiness, Saab was a cult favorite until its unfortunate demise in 2011.

Relations: Once partnered with GM, Saab used shared platforms with Opel and Chevrolet during the 2000s—but kept its distinct personality and dashboard-mounted ignition switch.

Most Famous Car Models of Saab:

The Saab 900 Turbo became a legend for blending performance with practicality. Its turbocharged engine and hatchback design made it unique, and it symbolized Saab’s engineering creativity.

A compact executive car that offered turbocharged fun and safety. The 9-3 marked Saab’s modern evolution, mixing sleek Scandinavian design with aircraft-inspired interiors.

The 9-5 was Saab’s flagship, offering more luxury and space while maintaining quirky touches like the night panel and wraparound dash. Its final-gen version remains a cult classic.
