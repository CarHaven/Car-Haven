Acura

History: Acura is the luxury and performance division of Honda, launched in 1986. It was the first Japanese luxury car brand, introducing vehicles that combined reliability with high-end appeal. Acura's early success in the U.S. market pushed Toyota and Nissan to launch their own luxury brands—Lexus and Infiniti.

Relations: Acura is a sub-brand of Honda and shares many platforms, engines, and technologies. Several Acura models are rebadged or upgraded versions of Honda vehicles, especially in terms of engineering and design philosophy.

Most Famous Car Models of Acura:

The NSX is Acura’s flagship supercar—sleek, hybrid-powered, and loaded with precision handling and futuristic design.

A sport sedan that blends luxury with performance. Available in powerful Type S variants with sharp styling and tech features.

A luxury SUV with three-row seating and premium features. Known for its comfort, reliability, and spaciousness.
