Acura

History: Acura is the luxury and performance division of Honda, launched in 1986. It was the first Japanese luxury car brand, introducing vehicles that combined reliability with high-end appeal. Acura's early success in the U.S. market pushed Toyota and Nissan to launch their own luxury brands—Lexus and Infiniti.

Relations: Acura is a sub-brand of Honda and shares many platforms, engines, and technologies. Several Acura models are rebadged or upgraded versions of Honda vehicles, especially in terms of engineering and design philosophy.

Most Famous Car Models of Acura:

The Acura NSX is a hybrid-powered supercar that blends electrified performance with classic driving thrills. With its aggressive stance, lightweight materials, and all-wheel-drive system, the NSX is a futuristic showcase of Honda engineering wrapped in luxury and precision.

The TLX is Acura’s take on the modern sport sedan — sharp styling, a tech-rich interior, and performance variants like the Type S that offer serious thrills. It’s comfortable enough for the daily grind but aggressive enough to remind you that driving can still be fun.

Luxury meets practicality in the Acura MDX. With three rows of seating, refined interior trims, and an available Super Handling All-Wheel Drive system, it’s a family SUV that doesn’t compromise on dynamics, making it both versatile and rewarding to drive.
