Cadillac

History: Cadillac was founded in 1902 and became the benchmark for American luxury. Known for innovation and status, it was the first U.S. car brand to win the Dewar Trophy for precision manufacturing.

Relations: Cadillac is a division of General Motors (GM), sharing platforms with other GM brands like Chevrolet and Buick, while leading GM’s push into electric luxury vehicles.

Most Famous Car Models of Cadillac:

The Escalade is Cadillac’s iconic full-size luxury SUV, known for its bold presence, high-tech interior, and celebrity appeal. It's a status symbol on wheels with plenty of power.

This performance sedan blends American muscle with refined luxury. With a supercharged V8 and track-tuned features, it’s a beast in a suit.

The Lyriq marks Cadillac’s electric future—sleek design, cutting-edge tech, and smooth electric performance. A quiet revolution wrapped in style.
