Fiat

History: Fiat (Fabbrica Italiana Automobili Torino) was founded in 1899 and quickly became Italy’s largest car manufacturer. Known for affordable city cars and quirky design, it played a vital role in motorizing Europe post-WWII.

Relations: Fiat is a core brand under Stellantis and has deep connections with Alfa Romeo, Jeep, Chrysler, and once Ferrari. It often shares platforms and technology with its sister brands.

Most Famous Car Models of Fiat:

An icon of Italy—compact, retro, and cute as hell. The 500 defined urban mobility and came back in style with the modern Fiat 500 series.

Practical, boxy, and reliable. The Panda is a beloved city workhorse, famous across Europe for its rugged simplicity.
