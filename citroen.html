Citroën

History: Citroën was founded in 1919 in France by André Citroën. Known for innovation, it introduced front-wheel drive and hydropneumatic suspension. The brand became synonymous with quirky but practical engineering.

Relations: Citroën is part of the Stellantis group, alongside Peugeot, Fiat, and Opel. It shares technology and platforms with these brands while maintaining a unique French identity.

Most Famous Car Models of Citroën:

The 2CV is a post-war French classic—minimal, affordable, and built to drive across fields with eggs in the trunk and none cracked. A true people’s car.

Legendary for its futuristic design and hydraulic suspension. The DS was so ahead of its time, it made other cars look ancient.

Modern, compact, and quirky—C3 carries the playful French vibe with bold design and customizable color combos. Popular in urban Europe.
