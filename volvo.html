Volvo

History: Volvo was founded in 1927 in Sweden and quickly built a rep for making some of the safest cars on the road. It introduced the three-point seatbelt in 1959 and gave it away for free — kingship behavior.

Relations: Volvo is now owned by Geely, a Chinese automotive group. Still, it maintains its Swedish roots, sharing some tech with Polestar and pushing hard toward a fully electric future.

Most Famous Car Models of Volvo:

The XC90 is Volvo's luxury SUV flagship—safe, sleek, and packed with understated power. It’s like driving a fortress wrapped in IKEA aesthetics.

The S60 blends Nordic design with sporty grace. A sedan that whispers class but isn’t afraid to purr when needed. All-wheel drive meets inner peace.

The 240 is a boxy legend. Built like a tank, beloved by dads, and now ironically cool among vintage car enthusiasts. The blueprint for Volvo's safe legacy.
